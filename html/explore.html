Marine Life Encyclopedia

Discover the great variety of underwater sea life that will blow your mind !

OCEAN FISHES

World oceans are the home for estimated 20,000 species of fish. They varied from different sizes, colors, breeds, some even live in extreme depth and are able to survive under freezing temperatures. Some common example of fishes are Clownfish, Cod, Salmon, Bluefin Tuna, Atlantic Grouper, Sturgeon and Halibut.

SHARKS

Sharks are ocean's top predator for hundreds of millions of years. There are currently 450 species of sharks with ranges of 8 inches long to 40 feet long. Usually found in deep and shallow waters throughout the oceans, however, the annual human casualties caused by sharks are only 72 cases, which is considered as significally lower than average. Common found sharks are Great White Shark, Tiger Shark, Angel Sharks, and Hammerhead Shark.

CRUSTACEANS and SHELLFISH

All crustaceans and shellfish are easily distinguished by their unique shells, which some of them are either soft or hard. Some of them has odd shapes and postures. However, they are a major part in maintaining healthy ocean systems. The most common example of crustaceans and shellfish are Crabs, Lobsters, Squid, Cuttlefish, Octopus and Shrimp.

SEA TURTLE

Sea turtle has lived side by side with dinosaurs in the earth, which is why this species is considered one of the oldest living animals in the earth. Sea turtle are characterized by their hard, long, streamlined shells covering most part of their body. Their colors are usually green, yellow, reddish-brown or dark green. Example of sea turtles are Flatback Turtle, Green Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle.

MARINE MAMMALS

Marine mammals, commonly known are whales, dolphins, and sea otters. Marine mammals are considered one of the smartest species of marine animals. The differences between marine mammals with common fishes are their way of breathing. Marine mammals uses air and lungs to breath, just like humans do. In order to grasp for air, marine mammals would come to the surface at certain time to get the air necessary for their breathing.
